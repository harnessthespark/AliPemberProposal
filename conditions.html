A day in your life

List the things you actually did today, or a typical day. For each, mark how much was care for you, and how much was care for someone else. The columns can both be high — or both low. Both are useful to notice.

~

One row at a time. The dots are 1 to 5 — tap to mark, tap again to unmark. Don't think too hard.

Questions to sit with

Open the day out and notice what comes up. None of these need answering now — they're for slow reading.

i.

Are the scores evenly divided across the two columns?

ii.

On a particular activity, do self-care and care-for-others match — or differ?

iii.

How do you feel about the balance between the two? Contented, neutral, or uneasy?

iv.

How do high or low-scoring activities relate to your energy? Tiring, exciting, refreshing?

v.

Can you notice bracing, reaching, or settling when you do certain things?

vi.

Are there particular stress reactions or inner patterns that keep showing up?

vii.

Is an inner critic, or an inner helper, in the room with you?

viii.

What about deeper motivations — me-first, you-first, or we-together? In line with your values?

ix.

If you can't change what you do, can you look at how you do it? What intentions might help?
